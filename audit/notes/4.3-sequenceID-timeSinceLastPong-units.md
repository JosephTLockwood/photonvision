## Sentinel inconsistency (NIT ã not catalogued)

`timeSinceLastPong` uses **two different "no-data" sentinels** depending on construction
path:

- Default-ctor at
  [PhotonPipelineMetadata.java:48](photon-targeting/src/main/java/org/photonvision/targeting/PhotonPipelineMetadata.java#L48):
  `Long.MAX_VALUE` ã semantically "infinitely long since last pong".
- Server-mode at
  [TimeSyncManager.java:137-142](photon-core/src/main/java/org/photonvision/common/dataflow/networktables/TimeSyncManager.java#L137-L142):
  `return 0` ã semantically "I AM the server, no pong needed".
- Client-mode: actual elapsed ôçs since most recent pong.

A consumer reading `timeSinceLastPong = 0` cannot tell whether (a) the robot is itself
the time-sync server, (b) a pong just arrived this instant, or (c) the field defaulted
to 0 somewhere along the way. The published value happens to mean the same thing in
practice ("you don't need to worry about pong freshness"), so the ambiguity is harmless.

The `// ????` comment in TimeSyncManager.java:140 next to the unknown-mode `return 0`
suggests the original author was uncertain too.

NIT. Not catalogued.
